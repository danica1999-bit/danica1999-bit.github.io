MY TOP 5 FAVORITE KDRAMA!

“Last episode promise!”

Maybe you also say it to yourself every time you watch kdrama. The kilig moments that you probably never forget and the lesson that it taught to us. So as your Unnie, here are my Top 5 kdrama favorites that you must watch and will surely make you cry, laugh, and fall in love.

PS. Park Seo Joon is mine. Lol 

OH MY GEUM-BI

This drama will surely give you a bucket of tears from episode 1 until the last. It is about a father and daughter relationship of how they managed to pass all the problems in their life. Hwi Chul is a conman artist and didn’t know that he has a daughter, and it is Geum Bi. Until they met, their crazy bickering to each other gives a light spot to the drama, when Hwi Chul accepts the fact that Geum Bi is her daughter, then sad news came it is about Geum Bi disease calls “childhood Alzheimers.” I’m amazed by how Hwi Chul changed his life for his daughter’s sake and how he struggles to support the treatment for Geum Bi disease.

I will rate this drama 10 out of 10; it is worth watching from the start until the end. It shows the love of a parent to their child, the chances for change, and being optimistic in life.

IT'S OKAY NOT TO BE OKAY

This drama is incredible, from the characters, dialogue, and storyline. It is a healing romance drama; where they deal with their past. Gang Tae is a worker in a psychiatric hospital; he has a brother named Sang Tae with autism. His brother idolizes the children’s book writer Ko Moon Young that has Antisocial Personality Disorder. This drama revolves around how each character faces the things they are scared of and what happened in their past.

I love how each character has their highlight of how they managed to get over the things they were scared of. The funny small fight between Sang Tae and Ko Moon Young, the humorous punch line of Ko Moon Young to Sang Tae is one of a kind. The character of Sang Tae in this drama touched me, his genuine love for his brother is incredible, and how much he wants to be a good older brother Gang Tae even, he is autistic.  

'It's Okay Not To Be Okay' by the title itself gives the greatest lesson in this drama. We are not all perfect, though love and acceptance should be given.
